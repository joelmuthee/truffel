Lipoabdominoplasty:
Combined Sculpting

The gold-standard combination procedure — liposuction plus tummy tuck for comprehensive abdominal transformation.

Duration: 3–5 Hours
Type: General Anaesthesia
Recovery: 4–6 Weeks

Procedure Overview

What Is Lipoabdominoplasty?

Lipoabdominoplasty is an advanced combination procedure that integrates liposuction with a traditional abdominoplasty (tummy tuck). Unlike a standard tummy tuck performed alone, lipoabdominoplasty uses tumescent liposuction to sculpt the flanks, upper abdomen, and waist simultaneously — resulting in a more comprehensive and naturally contoured transformation.

The technique is designed to preserve the blood vessels that supply the abdominal skin flap, specifically the perforating vessels from the rectus abdominis muscle. By maintaining this vascular network, Dr. Matwa is able to perform more extensive tissue mobilisation while significantly reducing the risk of wound healing complications — a major limitation of earlier combined approaches.

Lipoabdominoplasty is ideal for post-bariatric patients, post-pregnancy mothers, or individuals who have lost significant weight and present with both excess skin and resistant fat deposits that diet and exercise cannot address. The result is a flatter, more defined abdomen with improved waist definition and, in many cases, removal of lower abdominal stretch marks.

With stable weight maintenance, results are permanent. The fat cells removed do not return, and the repositioned tissue retains its new position.

How It Works

The Surgical Process

1

Assessment & Marking

Dr. Matwa conducts a thorough pre-operative assessment including body composition analysis, skin laxity evaluation, and abdominal wall examination. Precise surgical markings are made to map the liposuction zones, skin excision boundaries, and new umbilical position.

2

Tumescent Liposuction Phase

Tumescent fluid is infiltrated into the target zones — flanks, waist, and selected abdominal areas. Liposuction is performed using fine cannulas to sculpt the contour before skin resection, preserving the critical perforator vessels that supply the abdominal flap.

3

Skin Excision & Muscle Tightening

The lower abdominal skin is excised along the pre-marked incision. The rectus abdominis muscles are plicated (sutured together) to repair diastasis and restore a firm abdominal wall. The umbilicus is repositioned through a new opening in the elevated skin flap.

4

Closure & Compression

The wound is closed in layered sutures to minimise tension. Surgical drains may be placed if indicated. A medical-grade compression garment is fitted to support the new contour, reduce swelling, and assist with skin retraction during healing.

What to Expect

Recovery Timeline

Day
1–3

Initial Rest

Bed rest with pillows positioned under the knees to reduce tension on the abdominal closure. Drains managed if placed. Pain is well-controlled with prescribed analgesia.

Wk
1–2

Early Mobility

Gentle walking is encouraged from day 2–3 to promote circulation. Compression garment worn continuously. Follow-up visit for drain removal and wound assessment.

Wk
2–4

Light Activity

Light daily activities resumed — desk work, short drives, personal care. Swelling and bruising progressively resolve. Continue to avoid lifting anything heavier than 4kg.

Mo
2–3

Full Results

Return to exercise and full activity. Residual swelling resolves and final contour becomes visible. Scar continues to mature and fade over 12–18 months.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

A standard abdominoplasty removes excess skin and tightens abdominal muscles, but does not address fat deposits in the flanks or upper abdomen. Lipoabdominoplasty combines tumescent liposuction with the tummy tuck to sculpt the entire midsection in one operation. The result is a more comprehensive contour — a flatter abdomen plus a defined waist — that a tummy tuck alone cannot achieve. Crucially, the technique uses a vessel-preserving approach that maintains safety while allowing this more extensive scope of surgery.
Yes. Candidates should be at or near their goal body weight — ideally within 5–10kg — and have maintained that weight for at least 3–6 months. Significant weight gain after surgery will stretch the skin and alter the contour achieved. Lipoabdominoplasty is not a weight-loss procedure; it is a body-contouring procedure designed to remove residual excess skin and fat that persists despite reaching a healthy weight.
The primary incision runs horizontally in the lower abdomen, typically placed low enough to be hidden beneath underwear or a swimsuit. There is also a small incision around the new belly button position. Liposuction access incisions are 3–4mm and virtually invisible at maturity. Scars improve significantly over 12–18 months with proper scar management — silicone gel, sun protection, and hydration. Dr. Matwa discusses incision placement and scar management in detail at your consultation.
Pregnancy after lipoabdominoplasty is medically possible, but it is strongly recommended to complete your family before undergoing the procedure. A subsequent pregnancy will stretch the repaired abdominal muscles and skin, potentially reversing the surgical result. If further pregnancies are planned, Dr. Matwa may advise postponing the procedure until your family is complete to preserve the outcome long-term.
Initial results are visible immediately, though swelling masks the final contour for several weeks. By months 2–3, the majority of swelling has resolved and the new shape is well-defined. Final results, including scar maturity and complete resolution of minor swelling, are typically achieved at 6–12 months. Patients with a BMI under 30 and good skin elasticity tend to see the most dramatic early results.
